FAQ

  • What is Kickball?

    Learned by many North Americans in primary school, Kickball is played on a baseball diamond with a rubber ball. In place of a baseball bat, players used their feet to kick the ball from home plate, and attempt to run counterclockwise around the bases, while the opposing team tries to get them out by tagging them or the base ahead of the play.

    If this still makes no sense, no worries- We run a clinic ahead of the season to try to familiarize you. Plus there are tons of people who will help you learn.

  • What about Covid?

    We abide by local Dutch regulations in regards to the number of people that can assemble outside, etc. We try to make every effort to play, but will keep everyone in the loop if anything changes, including game dates, etc.

    We ask everyone to take personal responsibility when it comes to covid exposure. We respect everyone’s privacy, so keep in mind that a fellow player may be more at risk than you are- If you think you might have covid, please take a self test & stay home if it is positive. 

    If you travel & a quarantine upon return is required, please adhere to it.

    If you test positive within 3-5 days from being at a game, please alert the league Commissioners, as well as your team mates. If you wish to remain anonymous, please contact the League Commissioners, and we will contact the necessary parties, keeping your identity private. We ask for mutual respect for all parties.

  • Do I need any skill to join?

    You don’t need to know how to even kick a ball to join the league. We will guide you through the game rules and there are plenty of members that are always happy to help you out if you are a beginner.

  • Where and when do we play?

    Games happen on Saturday in Rembrandt park. The location of the games is sent out at the beginning of the seasons as well as the game schedule. You can expect games to start at the same time every week.

  • Do I need to bring anything to the games?

    We will provide you with your team shirt on opening day (or first day you show up). Other than that, we recommend you to bring sports shoes, a rain jacket (hey! it’s the Netherlands after all) and maybe a snack and some drinks if you want. Cleats are allowed as long as they’re not metal ones – these can ruin the bases pretty quick.

  • What is the age range/average of the league?

    While a good chunk of the league is in their 30s, we have people from all ages, from university students in their early 20s until people in their 50s. Only requirement is to be over 18.

  • Can I play on the same team as my friend?

    When you sign up, the form allows you to request to be on the same team as one other player…so choose wisely! We make every effort to honor this request, but it’s not always possible, so it’s time to make some new friends!

  • I signed up but don't use tikkie or have a Dutch bank account. Now what?

    Send us an email. We will help figure out the best way to get your payment. You are not allocated to a team until you are paid, so we don’t want to delay that.

  • I played on the Navy team last year, can I be on the Navy team again?

    Teams are re-allocated each season. You might get lucky and get on the same color  team again. If this is happens, awesome. You have the choice to get a fresh t-shirt. If you don’t need or want a new t-shirt, cool, we re-allocate those funds towards equipment and the end of season party.

  • I can only play a few games - Is that ok?

    Totally! The fee remains the same regardless of how many games you play. Some players only play one or two games per season, so we ask you to try & fill out the signup forms as accurately as possible. Attendance is not mandatory, but if you do sign up we hope you can show up as much as possible. Your team is counting on you and it’s never fun to forfeit.

  • The season has already started, can I still play?

    Message us & we will see. We try & make it happen when we can, but also can’t make promises, so don’t wait too long!

  • My friend is in town. Can they play?

    Sorry, to prevent ringers we don’t allow this to happen. But your friend should still come! We love good cheering sections. We are a SOCIAL league as well.

  • I got hurt during a game...

    That’s a bummer & we hope you are ok! You do play at your own risk…remember all those blurbs you agreed to when you signed up? This is where you said cool.
